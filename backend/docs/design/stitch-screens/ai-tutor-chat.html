Can you explain how photosynthesis works in plants? I'm specifically curious about the light-dependent phase.

person
smart_toy

Great question! Photosynthesis is like a tiny solar power plant inside every green leaf. The light-dependent reactions are the first stage of this process.

It happens within the thylakoid membranes of the chloroplasts. Here's the simple breakdown:

  • Chlorophyll absorption: Sunlight hits chlorophyll molecules, exciting electrons.
  • Water Splitting: The energy is used to split water (H₂O) into oxygen, which is released as a byproduct!
  • Energy Conversion: The process creates ATP and NADPH, which act as batteries for the next stage (the Calvin Cycle).

Question 1 — Standard Form 2.5 min

What is the vertex of the parabola defined by y = x² - 4x + 3?

A
(2, -1) check_circle
B
(-2, 1)
To find the vertex of y = ax² + bx + c, use x = -b/2a. Here, a=1 and b=-4, so x = -(-4)/2(1) = 2. Plug x=2 back into the equation: y = (2)² - 4(2) + 3 = 4 - 8 + 3 = -1. Therefore, the vertex is (2, -1).

Question 2 — Discriminants 4.2 min

If the discriminant (b² - 4ac) of a quadratic equation is negative, how many real roots does it have?

B
Two real roots Your Choice
C
Zero real roots check_circle
The discriminant determines the nature of the roots. If b² - 4ac < 0, the roots involve the square root of a negative number, resulting in two imaginary (complex) roots and zero real roots.

Question 3 — Factoring 1.5 min

Factor the expression: x² + 5x + 6

D
(x + 2)(x + 3) check_circle
We look for two numbers that multiply to 6 and add to 5. Those numbers are 2 and 3. Therefore, the factored form is (x + 2)(x + 3).
